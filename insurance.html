will your commercial dump truck insurance go up if someone hits you

Whether your commercial dump truck insurance goes up after an accident depends on your location, your insurer, and the circumstances of the incident. In most cases, if you’re not at fault, your insurance usually shouldn’t increase — but there are exceptions.

When Commercial Dump Truck Insurance Might NOT Go Up:

  • You’re clearly not at fault: If another driver caused the accident and their insurer pays, your premium generally stays the same.
  • You have accident forgiveness: Some insurers offer protection that prevents price increases after your first claim.
  • The claim is paid by the other driver’s insurer: If your company doesn’t pay, your policy is usually unaffected.

When Commercial Dump Truck Insurance MAY Increase:

  • Your insurer still pays the claim: For example, if the other driver is uninsured or can’t be identified.
  • Frequent claims history: Even if you’re not at fault, multiple claims can still affect your risk rating.
  • No-claim bonus impact: In some regions and insurers, your discount may still be reduced.

Insurance rules vary by provider, so it’s always best to check your policy terms. Many companies review your full driving and claims history, not just a single incident.

Tip: Always collect evidence after an accident — photos, police reports, and witness details — to clearly prove your dump truck wasn’t at fault.
