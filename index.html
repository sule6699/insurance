General Advice: What to Do in Common Cases

Car Accident +

If you're involved in a car accident, ensure your safety first, contact emergency services, and collect evidence.

Workplace Injury +

Report the incident, seek medical attention, and document everything for legal and insurance purposes.

Medical Malpractice +

If you suspect malpractice, get a second opinion and keep records of all treatments and communications.

Home Burglary +

Call the police, document damages, and notify your insurance company to start the claims process.

Identity Theft +

Freeze your credit, notify your bank, and report the theft to authorities as soon as possible.

Natural Disaster +

Follow evacuation orders, secure important documents, and stock up on emergency supplies.

Cyber Attack +

Change your passwords, enable two-factor authentication, and monitor accounts for unusual activity.

Lost Passport +

Report it immediately to the embassy and apply for a replacement as soon as possible.

Fraud/Scam +

Stop communication with the scammer, report the fraud to authorities, and alert your bank or credit card provider.

How Personal Injury Lawyers Get Paid & Client Compensation Breakdown

Personal injury lawyers typically work on a contingency fee basis, meaning they only get paid if they win the case. Their fee is a percentage of the final settlement or court award, usually ranging between 25% and 40%.

Breakdown of Payment Structure

  • Lawyer's Fee: Typically, 33.3% (or 1/3) of the total compensation is taken by the lawyer. However, this can vary:
    • 25% if settled early (before filing a lawsuit).
    • 33.3% if settled after filing but before trial.
    • 40% if the case goes to trial.
  • Client's Share: The client receives the remaining amount (60% to 75%) after the lawyer’s fee is deducted.
  • The client may also need to cover case expenses (court fees, expert witnesses, etc.), which are sometimes deducted separately.
